My Data Story

We're living in a society that uses digital technology and media to collect, organize, and analyze data about a multitude of ourselves. For my data story, I started with a projecct that challeneged me to learn how to produce digital media from my everyday life. I started off by collecting data, making a visual from that data and then and then went on to make an explained video that took my everyday data and connected it to something larger. This data story is meant to take something simple and have my audience think about it with a different perspective!

Data Collection

The main point of this data collection was to explore and gain insight into my writing practices and how digital media and data permeate it. I chose to collect my data on memes for one week. There were a lot of categories that went into this collection, such as the date/time, the app I was using, what I was using, where I was, etc. By doing this, I've learned to understand how digital media performs and communicates much more than what we may have originally thought. I personally learned that I look at way too many memes!

Data Visualization

After I collected my data, I analyzed it and developed categories and codes to visualize. I analyzed and explored my data to learn basic visualization conventions and through this, I made decisions about how I wanted to visualize my categories and their relationships and came up with a temporal chart. My chart is laid out the way it is so I could see the different times of day in each day going down the rows. My marks and attributes amplify these patterns because the same concept is seen throughout each day. It’s very easy to understand because once you learn how to read one box, it is easy to read the rest. I’ve given every attribute their own shape and the legend is what actually helps you read it. The colors are a very big part of reading this chart and everything is signified through the colors labeled in the legends! For example, After learning how to read this chart, my audience would see what social media apps I used the most. This may get them to think about why someone would use a certain app so much, which leads to the rules that they have to follow in order to use it - the larger idea it connects to my Explained Video below!

Explained Video

My video explores and explains issues linked to my data visual. I researched and created a short explanatory video about what can you learn about the data connected to your digital media practice. From my data visual, I decided to research End User Rights as it applied to my mostly. I've been using these apps so I wanted to know the rules and things I agreed to or gave up when I agreed to the social media's terms. I answered questions like: How do your own digital practices hook into business practices? And, what are your own opinions based on what you learned?
